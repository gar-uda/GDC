What is Forex?

Forex = Foreign Exchange. It’s the market where currencies are bought and sold in pairs, like EUR/USD.

Risk First

Most retail traders lose money. Learn risk management, demo trading, and psychology before using real money.

Practice

Use a broker’s demo account. Treat it like real money to build discipline.

Disclaimer: This site is for educational purposes only. Not financial advice. Forex trading involves substantial risk and is not suitable for everyone. Only trade with money you can afford to lose.

Lesson 1: Currency Pairs & Pips

Pair: EUR/USD means you buy EUR by selling USD. Pip: The smallest price move. For most pairs, 1 pip = 0.0001.

Lesson 2: Leverage & Margin

Leverage magnifies both gains and losses. High leverage is the #1 reason beginners blow accounts. Use low leverage while learning.

Lesson 3: Risk Management

Rule of thumb: Risk 1-2% per trade. Always use a Stop Loss. Never revenge trade after a loss.

Lesson 4: Technical vs Fundamental

Technical: Charts, support/resistance, indicators. Fundamental: Interest rates, news, economics. Most beginners start with technical + risk rules.
